# PRD: Tene360
**Version:** 1.0.0 | **Date:** January 2026 | **Status:** Initial Version

---

## The Core Problem Statement
For users of the **Black Network**, the process of identifying the right product/tool to handle a specific **[Entity]**, and understanding its full situational picture, is messy and scattered across different systems. This leads to **wasted time in searching, approaching the wrong product, and missing critical actions** that the user did not know were available to them.

---

## Root Cause Analysis

### 1. Lack of an "End-to-End User Journey" Concept in the Mobile Environment
Until today, the technological response in the Black Network was not designed with a holistic process-oriented view around the **[Entity]**. Instead of analyzing the full lifecycle of the [Entity] and all the interface points required for the user in the field, the organization left this arena completely empty. Now, with the understanding that various responses are required, there is a risk that each response will be developed separately without a guiding hand, which would recreate the problems of the past. The platform is required to prevent the creation of a "collection of isolated solutions" before they are even formed, and to ensure that all future technological responses connect into one continuous and logical process.

### 2. System-Centric Architecture instead of Entity-Centric Architecture
The organizational and technological world view is built around "Systems" (the Red System, the ERP system, etc.) and not around the central object (the **[Entity]**). As a result, information about the [Entity] is scattered, and there is no entity that centralizes its unified identity. The user is forced to maneuver between screens and systems to piece together a single picture.

### 3. A Security Paradigm that Fixates Dependency on the "Red Network"
Traditional security policy views the closed network as the only place for managing operational processes, which has created a barrier to developing solutions for the Black Network. Processes remained "trapped" in offices, and the gap between the operational need in the field and the technological capability deepened.

## Vision and Strategic Compass
The goal: To ensure that every feature developed serves the North Star.

> ### Vision Statement
> To establish the platform as the **exclusive Gateway** for all operational and technological responses for the **[Entity]** on the Black Network.
>
> The platform will centralize the variety of approved tools and processes under one roof, providing the user with a unified, professional, and secure work environment. In doing so, it will eliminate the need for local developments or the use of external civilian tools, ensuring that every future technological response integrates naturally as part of a complete and integrative organizational concept.

> ### Mission Statement
> To develop and implement a central work interface (**Hub**) on the Black Network, which performs unique identification of the **[Entity]**, and provides the user with immediate access to the basket of approved products, tools, and processes relevant to them, under a unified security and user experience envelope.

## Core Product Philosophies

### 1. Entity Centricity – "The [Entity] at the Center"
* **The Principle:** The navigation and information hierarchy will always start from the [Entity], not from the system or the module.
* **In Practice:** The user should not have to ask "Where is the reporting tool?", but rather search for the [Entity] and receive that tool as context. Every screen and every action must be associated with a specific [Entity].

### 2. Aggregation over Creation
* **The Principle:** The platform is the conductor of the orchestra, not the player. The goal is to centralize existing capabilities, not to duplicate them.
* **In Practice:** Before developing a new feature within the platform, we will check if a dedicated product exists in the ecosystem that knows how to do this, and we will prefer to perform a connection (Integration/Deep Link) to keep the platform lean and focused.

### 3. Zero Friction Entry
* **The Principle:** The path from the moment the user opens the platform until they see the action menu for the [Entity] must be the shortest and fastest possible.
* **In Practice:** No busy or complicated dashboards at entry, no endless settings menus. **Search -> Identify -> Action.** The user experience should be as intuitive as a Google search.

### 4. Security by Design – Secure by Default
* **The Principle:** Usability will never come at the expense of information leakage, but security will not be an excuse for a poor experience.
* **In Practice:** All information displayed is only that which is approved for exposure on the Black Network. We do not try to "bypass" limitations, but rather build processes that are pre-adapted to the permitted boundaries, to prevent a situation where the user returns to "WhatsApp" due to blockages.

## North Star Framework

### The North Star Metric
**"Weekly Productive Sessions"**
A session is considered "productive" if one of the following two occurs:
1.  The user clicked on an action tool (the ideal scenario).
2.  The user stayed on the [Entity] screen for over 15 seconds – an indication that they consumed significant information, such as a complex status.

## Product Strategy and Differentiation

### 1. Our Unfair Advantage
As an internal development body, we hold three strategic assets that no external party or off-the-shelf product can compete with in our specific operational environment:

* **Native Cross-Domain Capability:**
    The biggest barrier in the market is the transition between the Red Network (classified) and the Black Network. While external providers will struggle to cross the walls of security and regulation, we have direct access, familiarity with the internal information architecture, and the ability to create secure data pipelines tailored exactly to the logic of the [Entity].
* **Operational DNA and Intimate Familiarity:**
    We do not guess the requirements; we live the field. Our ability to translate a vague operational need ("I need to know what's happening with the [Entity]") into a precise technological solution is immediate. We do not have the "learning curve" of an external vendor who needs to learn the organizational language.
* **Data Trust:**
    Users and commanders trust internal systems as the "source of truth." Our ability to connect the platform directly to core systems without intermediaries ensures reliability that no external application will achieve.

### 2. The Strategic Equation: Why Internal Development? (Build vs. Buy)
The decision to develop the platform in-house instead of purchasing a task management system or a commercial portal stems from the following reasons:
* **Focus on the Object rather than Genericity:** Off-the-shelf systems like Monday or ServiceNow are generic. Adapting them to the complex model of the [Entity] and unique operational processes would require expensive and lengthy customization that would make them cumbersome. Internal development allows us to be **Tailor-Made** – 100% precise for the [Entity].
* **Security by Design:** Internal development allows us to embed the strictest partitioning and security requirements at the code and infrastructure level, rather than as a "patch" on an external product, enabling faster operational approval.

### 3. Adoption Strategy
How will we get users to abandon paper, Excel, and WhatsApp in favor of the platform?

* **A. The Convenience Wedge Strategy:**
    We will not force users to use the system ("the stick"); instead, we will entice them through value they don't have anywhere else ("the carrot").
    **The Tactic:** We will start by providing read-only information that is not available elsewhere. Once the user realizes that only on the platform can they see the true status of the [Entity] in seconds (instead of calling the office), they will enter. From there, the transition to performing actions (Write/Action) will be natural.
* **B. Creating the "Golden Path":**
    * We will position the platform as the exclusive pipeline for performing actions. Want to bring the [Entity] in for maintenance? Preliminary actions can only be performed through the platform (e.g., ammunition declaration).
    * Processes carried out using old methods (paper/WhatsApp) will receive lower priority in handling or be rejected outright, creating a natural incentive to move to digital.
* **C. Zero-Training Experience:**
    * The product will be so intuitive (an interface similar to familiar consumer apps) that pushing out old and complicated systems will be inevitable due to the sheer simplicity.

## Execution DNA

### 1. Ship to Learn
* **The Principle:** We do not wait for a perfect product. We launch the minimum viable product (MVP) that provides value to get feedback from the field as quickly as possible.
* **In Practice:** It is better to release the ability to view one central data point within two weeks than to wait six months for a full system. Every version must go out to the field, even if partial, to validate our assumptions against real users.

### 2. Connect, Don't Rebuild
* **The Principle:** Our role is integration, not reinventing the wheel. We fiercely avoid developing capabilities that already exist in other systems within the organization.
* **In Practice:** For every new feature, the first question in the development meeting will be: "Is there an existing API we can connect to?". If yes – we connect. We build from scratch ("Native") only when there is no other choice or when the existing experience is catastrophic.

### 3. Trust but Verify
* **The Principle:** When working with external systems (our Data Sources), we must be suspicious. We do not assume that information coming from the Red Network is always correct or available.
* **In Practice:** Developing fallback mechanisms. If the source system is down, the platform does not crash but displays a clear message ("Information is not up to date at the moment") or shows the last saved data (Cache) to maintain user trust.

### 4. Radical Simplicity
* **The Principle:** Technological complexity (connecting networks, encryptions, logic) must remain "under the hood." The user deserves zero cognitive effort.
* **In Practice:** We will measure the quality of execution by the number of clicks to perform an action. If a developer suggests a solution that requires complicated settings or 3 screens from the user – the solution will be rejected, even if it is easier to develop. The technological burden is on us, not the user.

## User Profile and Personas
This is a critical chapter that will help the development team understand the "vibe" of the product (UI/UX), the language it speaks, and the permitted level of complexity. Since the product is operational, the personas must reflect the pressure and load in the field.

### Primary Persona
* **Name (Generic):** Staff Sergeant Daniel, Technician/Combat Soldier.
* **Role:** Directly responsible for the operation and readiness of the [Entity] in real-time.
* **Environment:** Field, mud, scorching sun or darkness, time pressure, fatigue.
* **Motivation (Goals):**
    * "I want to finish treating the [Entity] and go back to sleep/the mission."
    * "I want to know exactly what I have and what I'm missing, without getting tangled up."
    * "I need the system to work for me fast, now."
* **Pain Points and Fears:**
    * **Fear of Bureaucracy:** Hates filling out long, never-ending forms.
    * **Fear of Disconnection:** Worries that his report will "disappear" and not reach headquarters.
    * **Technical Frustration:** Has no patience for slow systems or screens that don't load.
* **Technological Capabilities:**
    * **Digital Native:** Lives on TikTok and WhatsApp. Used to fast, smooth, and intuitive interfaces. Has zero tolerance for outdated UX ("like the military's").
* **Implication for the Product:**
    Large buttons (**Fat Finger design**), short texts, minimum typing, clear visual feedback for every action.

### Secondary Personas
* **Name:** Captain Roi, Company Commander/Technical Officer.
* **Role:** Manages the activities of Daniel and 10 others like him.
* **Motivation:**
    * "I want to see that everyone is working and that no [Entity] is forgotten."
    * "I want to ensure the reports are reliable before I pass them up."
* **Pain Point:** Overwhelmed with information, afraid of losing control.
* **Implication for the Product:**
    He will use the same application but may look for more "approvals" or summary statuses. He needs high reliability in the data (Data Integrity).

### The "Negative Persona" (Who are we NOT building for?)
* **Data Analyst at Headquarters:** The one who sits in an air-conditioned office with two screens and wants to do a Pivot Table on the entire fleet. For him, there are desktop BI systems. We will not build complex filtering and segmentation tools in mobile for him.
* **System Admin:** The one who needs to define permissions and users. He will do this in the management system (Back Office), not in the field app.
